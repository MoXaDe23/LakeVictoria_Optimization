Flood-risk diagnostics were evaluated for Lake Victoria (2001–2021) using multi-threshold exceedance metrics at 12.5 m, 12.8 m (high-flood reference), and 13.0 m. At the 12.8 m threshold, observed lake levels exceeded the threshold on 452 days, while the baseline Agreed Curve policy exceeded on 0 days and the optimized rule exceeded on 389 days. In addition to exceedance frequency, flood severity (sum of meters above threshold across exceedance days) showed that the optimized policy produced a total severity of 115.92 m-days at 12.8 m relative to 162.74 m-days under observed conditions. Across thresholds, the optimized policy demonstrates improved flood-risk shaping (reduced peak severity and altered exceedance structure) compared to observed operations, while avoiding the excessive long-term drawdown behavior exhibited by the baseline policy.
